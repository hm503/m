Dr. Magdy Yacoub:Savior of hearts

Heart surgery icon

about of the doctor

Magdy Habib Yacoub (born November 16, 1935) is an Egyptian professor and prominent heart surgeon. He was born in the city of Belbeis, Sharkia Governorate, Egypt, to a Coptic Orthodox family, whose origins are from Minya.

Dr. Magdy Yaqoub is an international Egyptian heart surgeon, best known for his pioneering contributions in the field of heart surgery, especially heart transplantation. He is considered one of the most important figures in the field of medicine in the world.

Dr. Yaqoub is considered a role model for the new generation of doctors, and he has received many international awards and honors in recognition of his contributions to the field of medicine.

ashievements

He established the first center in Aswan to treat children with heart diseases, entitled the Magdy Yacoub Foundation for Cardiology

Magdy Yacoub contributed to saving the lives of many children in the world who were born with a congenital defect in the major blood vessels

He performed open-heart surgery on the English actor Eric Morecambe, and he also succeeded in performing another operation on the international actor Omar Sharif

He has a major role in developing cardiovascular surgery programs in the regions of the Arabian Gulf, Mozambique and Ethiopia

Foundations of heart transplant surgery in Britain

He established a charity called Chains of Hope with the aim of helping children born with congenital heart defects, and finding modern ways to save their lives
